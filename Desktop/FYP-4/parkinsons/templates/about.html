Parkinson's disease is a neurodegenerative disorder that affects millions of people worldwide. It is a chronic and progressive disease that affects movement, balance, and coordination, causing symptoms such as tremors, rigidity, and slowness of movement. Early diagnosis and treatment of PD are crucial for improving patients' quality of life, as well as slowing the progression of the disease.

Machine learning algorithms have been widely used in recent years to aid in the diagnosis and management of PD. ParkinSenze aim to develop a machine learning model for early detection of PD based on the analysis of various speech and motor-related features. The dataset used in this project consists of a large number of recordings of voice and movement signals from patients with and without PD.

The ultimate goal is to develop a robust and accurate machine learning model that can assist healthcare professionals in the early detection and management of PD, leading to better patient outcomes. The web application allows patients to provide their demographic information and voice samples, which are then analysed by the ML model. The web application provides patients with a diagnosis and recommends further action based on the diagnosis.

ParkinSenze aimed to develop a machine learning model for the detection of Parkinson's disease. Various ML classifiers, such as Random Forest, SVM, Naive Bayes, and Logistic Regression, on different datasets, including the UCI ML Parkinson's dataset and PPMI dataset were experimented. This model achieved promising results with, which includes significant improvement in accuracy over the existing methods. Additionally, a web application that can aid physicians in diagnosing Parkinson's disease accurately and quickly was developed.

The web application provides an easy-to-use interface for physicians to diagnose Parkinson's disease. The application takes input from the user in the form of patient data and generates a diagnosis based on our machine learning model. The app can also provide an explanation of the diagnosis to the physician. This web application can be a valuable tool for physicians in accurately diagnosing Parkinson's disease, especially in regions where specialized care is not available.
